Assignment 4

  1. Describe the backward algorithm. What is the meaning of βt(i) (see slides)?

    The path that was taken to find the product of the highest probabilities during the forward algorithm.

  2. Describe the forward algorithm. What is the meaning of αt(i)?

    The probability, at each step along the string of inputs, that one of the states was reached.

  3. How does the Baum-Welch algorithm estimate parameters?

    It estimates parameters by feeding its output back into the input parameters until (hopefully) the parameters converge on some values.

  5. What are restriction enzymes? Why do bacteria have restriction enzymes?

    Restriction enzymes are DNA cutters that recognize certain sequences and cut the DNA at that point. Bacteria have these enzymes as a defense mechanism against intruding viral DNA.

  9. What are episomes and how are they used to replicate human DNA?

    Episomes are sequences of circular DNA that are not core to the function of a bacteria. They can be snipped open so that "sticky" ends are revealed where they will match prepared complementary human DNA strands, and then ligated together. Once this foreign DNA has been introduced, the episomes will perform their replicative roles and often generate proteins as expected.

  10. Is it possible for bacteria to evolve pennicilin resistance during the last 50 years? How do you explain the development of MRSA?

    Yes, by selecting for bacteria that have developed resistant mutation, we encourage the further growth of these strains. MRSA is one example of such a strain (or strains).
